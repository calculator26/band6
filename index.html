📖 How the ATAR Calculator Works & Disclaimers ▾

The HSC ATAR is calculated by NESA using a two-step process. This calculator replicates both steps using Knox's actual data.

1️⃣ Units & Contributions

Each subject is worth either 2 units or 1 unit. Your KAM (Knox Assessment Mark, /50) is multiplied by the unit value to get your contribution. Extension subjects are 1 unit; most others are 2.

2️⃣ Best 10 Units

NESA counts only your best 10 units. If you study more than 10 units total, the subject with the lowest contribution is dropped. English is compulsory — always included regardless.

3️⃣ Aggregate Score

The contributions of your best 10 units are summed to give your aggregate, from 0 to 500. Higher aggregate = higher ATAR. The aggregate is simply KAM × units for each subject, totalled.

4️⃣ ATAR Lookup

The aggregate is converted to an ATAR using a state-wide conversion table. An aggregate of 400+ ≈ ATAR 94+, and 450+ ≈ ATAR 99+. Lookup covers aggregates 167–479. Results are rounded to nearest 0.05.

Step-by-Step Example
Step 1: Compute each subject's contribution: KAM × units
e.g. English Adv KAM 40 × 2u = 80 · Maths Adv KAM 42 × 2u = 84
Step 2: Rank contributions, keep best 10 units (drop lowest if >10u)
Step 3: Sum contributions of best 10 units → Aggregate (max 500)
Step 4: Look up aggregate in NSW conversion table → ATAR (rounded to nearest 0.05)
The ATAR Equivalent shown next to each subject = KAM × 10 (as if it were the only subject out of 500)
⚠️ Important Disclaimers: (1) Your KAM (Knox Assessment Mark) is an estimate — it reflects your current standing but will change as more tasks are assessed. (2) Your actual HSC mark is moderated against your school assessment rank and the state-wide performance of Knox students. (3) Scaling, moderation, and state-wide cohort variation mean your real ATAR will differ. (4) This is based on averages over multiple years — use for directional planning only, not as a prediction.
